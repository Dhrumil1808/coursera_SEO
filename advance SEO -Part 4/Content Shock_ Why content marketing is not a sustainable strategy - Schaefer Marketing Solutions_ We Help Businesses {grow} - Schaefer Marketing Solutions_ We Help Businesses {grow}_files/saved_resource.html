                                                                                                                    Don Purdum 2 years ago

                                                                                                                    Hi Mark,

                                                                                                                    You make some great points, but I also think there are incredible opportunities for us.

                                                                                                                    Here is what I mean... while we are creating more content today in 48 hours than in all of human history through 2003, the overwhelming majority of content is garbage that says nothing, means nothing and helps no one.

                                                                                                                    While it initially makes it hard to compete, in the long-run it creates incredible opportunity as we of offer what I call context marketing. Most marketing online is about "me" - that has to be flipped to be about the audience.

                                                                                                                    I have to know what business I'm really in (how does the public perceive me?), what problems do I "really" solve, who do I "specifically" solve them for and how do my products and services serve as a part of the solution?

                                                                                                                    We all have to pay for our marketing so I really like that you attach a value to your blogging and content marketing. The key metric for me is how much did it cost me and what is the return.

                                                                                                                    Because blogging and context marketing is first about establishing competency, it helps regardless of where someone comes to my site from. People want to know above all else if I'm competent to solve their problems. Relationship comes later, but it's always predicated on my competency. Just cost someone enough money for long enough and watch what happens to the relationship.

                                                                                                                    When done right, we will stand out far above and beyond and for me, well I've earned the opportunity to consult or coach small businesses and Fortune 1000's alike. The experience has been phenomenal and content / context marketing has saved my marketing budget and increased my ROI significantly because I understand how to use the tools and where to spend my time.

                                                                                                                    Is it for everyone? Absolutely not. That's where the rub comes in. Unless someone is willing to put in the training, time and investment to learn how to it right they will waste their time and in turn their money.

                                                                                                                    I really enjoyed your post Mark and I do think you're on to something. I believe it's an opportunity and call to action for us to change our approach based on the needs of ones target audience.

                                                                                                                    I hope you have a great week ahead and I do hope to engage with you!

                                                                                                                    ~ Don Purdum
